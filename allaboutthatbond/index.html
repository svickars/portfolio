Shaken, not stirred.

The World of James Bond
October 2015
Adobe Awards 2016, Semifinalist

This map is the result of my undying need to know everything James Bond - from the number of ladies he kisses in each film, to the cities he visits, to the cars he drives. This map shows it all and the connections between each. To view a high-quality PDF, click here, or scroll down to zoom.

Shaken, not stirred.

Ever wonder which James Bond made the most money? How about which actor flirted with Moneypenny the most? Or who killed the most henchmen? I have. So I did what anybody would do: I sat down with a martini and watched every 007 film (including the unofficial Never Say Never Again), and I got to know every actor, every recurring character, every gadget, every car, and every killer pun. Then I made this.

The name's Bond.

The classic 007 intro is just one of many Bondisms that I tallied and tracked to make this map. Others include martinis and units of alcohol consumed (Daniel Craig is by far the booziest James, drinking 24 units in Casino Royale); number of women kissed (that record goes to Roger Moore, kissing 27 women over just seven appearances); kills (see Roger Moore with 64 in Octopussy or Pierce Brosnan with 34 in GoldenEye) and killer puns (Sean Connery had eleven over seven outings); and times believed to be dead (Roger fooled his foes three times in A View to a Kill). I also looked at length, the longest being Spectre at 148 minutes, adjusted budget, the highest being Quantum of Solace at $257 million US, and adjusted gross earnings, the highest earner being 1965's Thunderball until Skyfall took over in 2012.

Shocking. Positively shocking.

The four Daniel Craig films realized the potential of the 007 universe, revealing it as one of the most in-depth and widespread in film. The World of James Bond is one of the most in-depth and widespread visualizations ever made about a single film franchise. I studied each film carefully, with my finger on the pause button and my laptop open to vehicle, weapon, and gadget databases, tracking every recurring character (those that have appeared in more than one film - remember Jaws and Sheriff Pepper?), every location he visited (the most common country outside of England being the United States) and the entirety of Q-Branch (see gadgets, weapons, and vehicles, like that one time Timothy Dalton rode a cello case in The Living Daylights). The map also shows motifs and themes, organizations mentioned, and endings (Sean Connery ended six of seven outings at sea, while Daniel Craig is the only Bond to take a villain into custody).

Sheer magnetism, darling.

The final print is 60″x36″ on fine art paper. One colour is assigned to each Bond actor, while a shade of that colour is given to each movie. You can follow a thread from each film through characters, locations, gadgets, vehicles, motifs, and endings. If you’d like to order a print, don’t hesitate to get in touch.
