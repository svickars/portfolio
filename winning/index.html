Since 1870, 311 distinct professional sports franchises have existed in North America1. In 2017, 1917 NCAA Div I teams represented 352 colleges and universities in women's basketball, soccer, and volleyball, and men's football, basketball and baseball2. In all, these teams have represented 154 cities across Canada and the United States and have competed for 1000 titles since 1870.

Here, I look at the last 148 years of sport to find North America's Titletowns — its winningest cities, the places who have known the most athletic success, the capitals of championship.

Scroll on to find them for yourself.

We're going to start in 1870 and as we move down the page, we'll move forward, year by year, until 2018. As we go, a coloured circle will appear on the map to the right for every city that has won a title. For every championship a city wins, the circle gets bigger. You'll also see a trophy follow the winning teams for the six professional sports leagues represented, and an emoji representing the five NCAA sports.
